Protect Yourself from Dengue

Dengue is a mosquito-borne disease affecting millions in India each year. Understanding symptoms and taking simple preventive steps can save lives.

What is Dengue?

  • Transmitted by the Aedes aegypti mosquito, which is most active during daylight hours
  • Symptoms typically appear 4–10 days after being bitten
  • Outbreaks peak during and immediately after the monsoon season when stagnant water is prevalent

Symptoms to Watch For

High Fever

Sudden onset up to 40°C / 104°F

Severe Headache

Intense, often behind the eyes

Eye Pain

Pain or pressure behind the eyes

Muscle & Joint Pain

Severe body aches, sometimes called "breakbone fever"

Nausea & Vomiting

Persistent nausea, sometimes with vomiting

Skin Rash

Flat red rash appearing 2–5 days after fever onset

Prevention Tips

Drain stagnant water

Empty pots, tires, flower vase dishes, and gutters. Mosquitoes breed in still water.

Use mosquito nets

Sleep under treated nets — even during daytime rest periods.

Apply mosquito repellent

Use DEET-based repellent on exposed skin, especially when outdoors.

Cover exposed skin

Wear long-sleeved shirts and full-length trousers, especially at dawn and dusk.

Install window screens

Keep windows and doors fitted with fine-mesh screens or keep them closed.

Keep surroundings clean

Regular waste disposal and clearing overgrown vegetation reduces breeding sites.

Be alert at dawn & dusk

Aedes mosquitoes are most active in the early morning and late afternoon hours.

Store water safely

Cover overhead tanks, drums, and buckets tightly. Change water in coolers at least once a week.

Seek Immediate Medical Attention

Go to casualty or call emergency services if you notice any of these warning signs:

  • Severe abdominal pain or tenderness
  • Persistent vomiting (3+ times in 24 hours)
  • Bleeding from nose or gums
  • Blood in vomit or stools
  • Extreme fatigue or unusual restlessness
  • Difficulty breathing or rapid breathing
